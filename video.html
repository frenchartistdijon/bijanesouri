Director, "LMK" 

Directed, designed, shot, and edited "LMK" for Lerado Khalil.

This was my first time working with video as a medium. The visuals are a juxtaposition of organic visual noise to solid geometric structures, as well as juxtaposing bright colors with muted neutral colors. Aesthetically I was inspired by a book I read on mosaics as well as works by John Baldessari where he painted brightly colored circles over black and white photography. The bright colors in the video complement the much more neutral dull colors to enhance each shot. The physical frames in the video were intended to capture the idea of "knowing." When I first heard the song I was conceptualizing the idea of being in the uknown, which is visually represented by the landscape. The physical frames are there to give meaning and structure to the unkown. To enhance the video without adding any effects, I placed still mosaics that look like color palettes in different parts of the video. The videos themselves are composed in a mosaic collage form. Combining the motion with the still solid structures, I was able to find a complementary relationship between the video movement to the still shots.
